NOTES: 72 Economies of scale  refer to the impact of a higher rate of output on  long run  average costs. cost elasticity  wrt  to output > 1 if diseconomies of scale cost elasticity  wrt  to output < 1 if economies of scale   So economies of scale is a cost-elasticity concept. Increasing returns to scale are a technological concept – relating quantity of output to quantity of inputs. Increasing returns to scale are a factor in bringing about economies of scale, but it’s not the only factor. You could have constant returns to scale and still have economies of scale if the cost of factors falls as you purchase more factors – for instance by buying materials in bulk quantities, at a discount. You could have constant returns to scale and diseconomies of scale if you have to pay more for more inputs – technologically you have CRS (or even IRS) but you may suffer diseconomies of scale if your demand for labor and capital shifts up the price of capital and labor (you are not a price taker). Summary: Economies of scale are not the same as increasing returns to scale.  E(dis) conomies  of scale may exist because input prices change with the output produced.
